Customized weight management

Semaglutide slows gastric emptying, prolonging the digestion process and resulting in reduced calorie absorption from meals. This contributes to decreasing overall calorie intake, fostering sustained weight loss when complemented by proper dietary habits and physical activity.

Semaglutide can benefit metabolism by improving insulin sensitivity and regulating blood sugar levels. By addressing underlying factors contributing to weight gain, such as appetite control and metabolic improvements, semaglutide supports lasting results when combined with lifestyle changes like exercise and a healthy diet.

Important Safety Information

Active Ingredients: Semaglutide (base)

Common side effects seen in less than 5% of patients include:

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Decreased appetite
  • Vomiting
  • Constipation
  • Dyspepsia
  • Abdominal pain

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

Rare, but serious side effects may include:

  • Hypoglycemia
  • Hypersensitivity reactions

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

It’s important to share your entire medical history with your provider. In particular, let your provider know if you have or have had:

  • MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Type 1 or type 2 diabetes
  • Thyroid cancer
  • Pancreatitis
  • Kidney disease
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Depression
  • Suicidal thoughts or behavior
  • Are pregnant, may become pregnant, or are breastfeeding

Tell your healthcare provider about all the medicines you take. Including prescription and over-the-counter medications, vitamins, and herbal and dietary supplements. Especially tell your provider if you take any of the following:

  • Oral birth control
  • Medications for type 1 or type 2 diabetes: insulin or sulfonylureas (e.g., Amaryl or Glucotrol XL)
  • Other GLP-1 medications: Ozempic, Wegovy, Saxenda, Victoza, Byetta, or Bydureon
  • Other products for weight loss, including dietary supplements

This is not a complete list of possible side effects. If you notice other effects not listed above, newsletter your doctor or pharmacist. You may also report side effects to FDA at 1-800-FDA-1088 or at www.fda.gov/medwatch.
