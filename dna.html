Common questions about Epigenetic and DNA Testing

Epigenetic and DNA testing analyzes your genetic material to provide insights into your health, lifestyle factors, and potential disease risks. It can help you understand how your genes interact with your environment and behavior.

Testing is usually done via a saliva or blood sample, which is then analyzed in a certified laboratory. The process is non-invasive, simple, and safe.

Anyone interested in understanding their genetic predispositions, health risks, or personalized wellness strategies can benefit from epigenetic and DNA testing. It’s particularly useful for those looking to optimize diet, fitness, and preventive care.

Results are typically provided in an easy-to-read report highlighting your genetic traits, health risks, and actionable recommendations. Consulting with a healthcare professional or genetic counselor can help you understand and act on these insights effectively.

Important Safety Information

Active Ingredients: tirzepatide (base)

Common side effects seen in less than 5% of patients include:

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Decreased appetite
  • Vomiting
  • Constipation
  • Dyspepsia
  • Abdominal pain

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

Rare, but serious side effects may include:

  • Hypoglycemia
  • Hypersensitivity reactions

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

It’s important to share your entire medical history with your provider. In particular, let your provider know if you have or have had:

  • MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Type 1 or type 2 diabetes
  • Thyroid cancer
  • Pancreatitis
  • Kidney disease
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Depression
  • Suicidal thoughts or behavior
  • Are pregnant, may become pregnant, or are breastfeeding

Tell your healthcare provider about all the medicines you take. Including prescription and over-the-counter medications, vitamins, and herbal and dietary supplements. Especially tell your provider if you take any of the following:

  • Oral birth control
  • Medications for type 1 or type 2 diabetes: insulin or sulfonylureas (e.g., Amaryl or Glucotrol XL)
  • Other GLP-1 medications: Ozempic, Wegovy, Saxenda, Victoza, Byetta, or Bydureon
  • Other products for weight loss, including dietary supplements

This is not a complete list of possible side effects. If you notice other effects not listed above, contact your doctor or pharmacist. You may also report side effects to FDA at 1-800-FDA-1088 or at www.fda.gov/medwatch.
