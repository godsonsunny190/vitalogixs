Common questions about GLP-1s

Our GLP-1 weight loss treatments are both designed to support weight loss by helping regulate appetite and blood sugar. Tirzepatide tends to have a slightly greater effect than semaglutide, and as a result it also comes at a higher price. The right option for you depends on your health history, weight loss goals, budget, and whether you’re comfortable with needles.

GLP-1s work by mimicking a hormone that targets areas of the brain that regulate appetite. They help you feel full sooner and for longer periods, reducing cravings and overall calorie intake, leading to gradual and sustained weight loss.

While GLP-1s are generally deemed safe, potential side effects may include nausea, headache, and constipation. Note: Compounded medications are not reviewed or approved by the FDA for safety or effectiveness.

Results vary by individual, but most people begin noticing changes within the first few weeks, with more substantial weight loss occurring over the course of several months as the body adjusts to the treatment. Regular follow-ups with your healthcare provider are key to monitoring progress.

Important Safety Information

Active Ingredients: tirzepatide (base)

Common side effects seen in less than 5% of patients include:

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Decreased appetite
  • Vomiting
  • Constipation
  • Dyspepsia
  • Abdominal pain

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

Rare, but serious side effects may include:

  • Hypoglycemia
  • Hypersensitivity reactions

Tell your doctor if any of these symptoms are severe or do not go away.

It’s important to share your entire medical history with your provider. In particular, let your provider know if you have or have had:

  • MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Type 1 or type 2 diabetes
  • Thyroid cancer
  • Pancreatitis
  • Kidney disease
  • Diabetic retinopathy
  • Depression
  • Suicidal thoughts or behavior
  • Are pregnant, may become pregnant, or are breastfeeding

Tell your healthcare provider about all the medicines you take. Including prescription and over-the-counter medications, vitamins, and herbal and dietary supplements. Especially tell your provider if you take any of the following:

  • Oral birth control
  • Medications for type 1 or type 2 diabetes: insulin or sulfonylureas (e.g., Amaryl or Glucotrol XL)
  • Other GLP-1 medications: Ozempic, Wegovy, Saxenda, Victoza, Byetta, or Bydureon
  • Other products for weight loss, including dietary supplements

This is not a complete list of possible side effects. If you notice other effects not listed above, contact your doctor or pharmacist. You may also report side effects to FDA at 1-800-FDA-1088 or at www.fda.gov/medwatch.
